# Skill: Workout Generator

Provides workout plan templates, rep/set schemes, periodization models, and progression logic for the Health & Nutrition Coach agent.

---

## Fitness Level Definitions

| Level | Description |
|---|---|
| **Beginner** | <6 months consistent training, learning movement patterns |
| **Intermediate** | 6 months–2 years, solid form, some stalls |
| **Advanced** | 2+ years, periodization needed, diminishing returns |

## Rep/Set Schemes by Goal

### Strength (max force output)
- Sets: 3–5
- Reps: 1–5
- Rest: 3–5 minutes
- Intensity: 80–95% 1RM
- Frequency: 3–4x/week per muscle group (lower volume)

### Hypertrophy (muscle growth)
- Sets: 3–5
- Reps: 6–12
- Rest: 60–90 seconds
- Intensity: 65–80% 1RM
- Frequency: 2–3x/week per muscle group

### Muscular Endurance
- Sets: 2–4
- Reps: 15–25
- Rest: 30–60 seconds
- Intensity: 50–65% 1RM

### Fat Loss / Conditioning
- Circuit or HIIT format
- Work:Rest ratio 1:1 (beginner) to 2:1 (advanced)
- 3–5 rounds of 4–6 exercises
- Keep heart rate 70–85% max HR

### General Fitness / Maintenance
- Sets: 3
- Reps: 8–15
- Rest: 60–90 seconds
- Mix compound and isolation

## Training Splits

### 2 Days/Week (Beginner or Maintenance)
**Full Body A/B**
- Day A: Squat pattern, horizontal push, horizontal pull, core
- Day B: Hinge pattern, vertical push, vertical pull, carry

### 3 Days/Week (Intermediate)
**Push / Pull / Legs**
- Push: Chest, shoulders, triceps
- Pull: Back, biceps, rear delts
- Legs: Quads, hamstrings, glutes, calves

**Or: Upper / Lower / Full Body**

### 4 Days/Week (Intermediate–Advanced)
**Upper/Lower Split**
- Upper A (strength focus) / Lower A (strength)
- Upper B (hypertrophy) / Lower B (hypertrophy)

### 5–6 Days/Week (Advanced)
**PPL x2** or **Bro split** (chest/back/legs/shoulders/arms)
> ⚠️ Only for advanced lifters with solid recovery capacity

## Bodyweight-Only Programs

### Beginner Bodyweight (3x/week, Full Body)
```
Warm-up: 5 min (jumping jacks, arm circles, leg swings)

Circuit (3 rounds, 60s rest between rounds):
1. Squat × 12
2. Push-up × 8 (knee push-up if needed)
3. Glute bridge × 15
4. Inverted row or band row × 10
5. Dead bug × 8 each side
6. Walking lunge × 10 each leg

Cool-down: 5 min static stretch
```

### Intermediate Bodyweight (3x/week, Push/Pull/Legs)
```
Push Day:
- Push-up 4×12, Pike push-up 3×8, Tricep dip 3×10, Decline push-up 3×10

Pull Day:
- Pull-up 4×6 (or band-assisted), Inverted row 3×10, Band face pull 3×15, Bicep curl (band) 3×12

Leg Day:
- Bulgarian split squat 3×10 each, Romanian deadlift (band) 3×12, Glute bridge 3×20, Calf raise 4×20, Jump squat 3×8
```

## Progression Models

### Linear Progression (Beginner)
Add weight every session (or every 2 sessions for upper body).
- Lower body: +5 kg/session
- Upper body: +2.5 kg/session
- Bodyweight: add 1 rep per set per week, then advance to harder variation

### Double Progression (Intermediate)
Train within a rep range. When you hit the top of the range for all sets, increase weight.
- Example: 3×8–12. When you do 3×12, add 2.5–5 kg next session.

### Wave Loading (Advanced)
Cycle intensity across weeks:
- Week 1: 3×8 @ 70%
- Week 2: 4×6 @ 75%
- Week 3: 5×4 @ 80%
- Week 4: Deload (3×5 @ 60%)

### RPE-Based (Advanced)
Rate of Perceived Exertion (1–10 scale):
- Strength work: RPE 8–9 (1–2 reps left in tank)
- Hypertrophy: RPE 7–8
- Volume work: RPE 6–7

## Injury Modifications

| Injury | Avoid | Substitute |
|---|---|---|
| Knee pain | Deep squats, lunges | Leg press (partial), step-ups, terminal knee extension |
| Lower back pain | Barbell deadlift, good mornings | Hip thrust, cable pull-through, bird dog |
| Shoulder impingement | Overhead press, upright row | Landmine press, neutral-grip press, face pulls |
| Wrist pain | Push-up (flat hand), front squat | Fist push-up, dumbbell press, goblet squat |
| Elbow tendinitis | Heavy curls, tricep dips | Hammer curl, band pushdown, light extensions |

## Recovery Guidelines

| Training Volume | Recovery Recommendation |
|---|---|
| 2–3 days/week | 1 active recovery day between sessions minimum |
| 4–5 days/week | Sleep 7–9h/night; deload every 4–6 weeks |
| 6 days/week | 1 full rest day; micro-deload every 3–4 weeks |

**Active recovery options:** Walking, light swimming, yoga, foam rolling, mobility work.

**Deload week:** Reduce volume by 40–50%, keep intensity similar. Purpose: connective tissue recovery, nervous system reset.

## Warm-Up Protocol

**General (5–10 min):**
1. 2–3 min light cardio (walk, bike, jump rope)
2. Dynamic mobility for joints involved in session
3. 2 warm-up sets of primary lift at 40–60% working weight

**Movement-specific:**
- Squat session: leg swings, hip circles, goblet squat, ankle mobilization
- Upper push: shoulder circles, band dislocations, chest opener
- Hinge: cat-cow, hip hinge drill, glute activation
